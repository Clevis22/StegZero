How It Works

What is Steganography?

  • Hiding a message inside another non-secret message or medium
  • Unlike encryption, the goal is to hide the existence of the message
  • This tool uses invisible Unicode characters as the hiding medium
  • The technique is called zero-width steganography

The Encoding Process

  • Your message is converted to UTF-8 bytes
  • A header with magic marker, nonce, length + CRC32 is prepended
  • Bits are mapped to 8 zero-width/invisible Unicode characters (3 bits each)
  • Hidden characters are evenly interleaved across the cover text

Limitations

  • Twitter/X and Facebook strip zero-width characters
  • WhatsApp may strip some — test before relying on it
  • Not secure — anyone who runs the decode will see your message
  • Not suitable for sensitive or confidential information

Good Use Cases

  • Hiding watermarks in shared text to track leaks
  • Easter eggs in documents or web pages
  • Puzzles and ARGs
  • Learning about Unicode and text encoding
FAQ

Is this secure?

  • No. It's obfuscation, not encryption. Anyone who pastes your text into this tool will see the message.
  • For real security, layer it with GPG/AES encryption first, then hide the ciphertext.

Which characters are used?

  • ZWSP (U+200B), ZWNJ (U+200C), ZWJ (U+200D)
  • Word Joiner (U+2060), Invisible Times (U+2062)
  • Invisible Separator (U+2063), Invisible Plus (U+2064)
  • Zero-Width No-Break Space / BOM (U+FEFF)

Is my data sent anywhere?

  • No. Everything runs in your browser via JavaScript.
  • No text is ever sent to a server.
  • The source code is open on GitHub.

Can I use it without cover text?

  • Yes — cover text is optional. The hidden characters will be output on their own.
  • But this makes detection trivial — always use a natural-looking cover text.
